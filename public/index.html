Account Creation Simulation

Background Information: You are creating an account for a shopping website. The only required field is username and you are free to include as many security measures as you want. As a shopping website you should imagine that your financial information MAY be linked to your account, however, you would also have the ability to remove it at any time. Furthermore, the shopping website would allow 3rd-party payment options that would need additional user engagement. Lastly, information concerning various security measures is included and can be accessed by clicking on buttons labeled "INFO", however, they are not required to be read.

  • Emails can be useful in terms of security in several of ways. One way is that they can be used in order to recover a lost or stolen account. Another way is that companies can warn users about any suspicious activity or data breaches. One last way is that emails are sometimes used as a form of multi-factor authentication

  • Phone numbers can be helpful in terms of security as you can receive SMS messages for multi-factor authentication

  • Passwords are a fundamental part of online security as they can lock unwanted individuals from accessing an account. Passwords can range in strength, depending on the characters used, complexity, and length. Generally, a strong password should be at least 8 - 12 characters, unique (not used for other accounts), have a mixture of both uppercase and lowercase letters, contain numbers, and contain at least one special character.

  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is an additional security measure that can help keep unwanted intruders from accessing an account. It does this by making the owner of the account confirm their identity through a secondary source. Some of these sources include a security key, authentication app, SMS, and email. Although beneficial, MFA can be inconvenient, due to requiring an extra step for sign-in. Furthermore, the different authenticators have different pros and cons.

    SMS and email are usually more easy to access, however there are larger risks if an individual gains access to either service. Furthermore, they have risks of phishing attacks.

    Authenticator apps are also easy to access, however if an individual loses access of their phone, they may have trouble logging into their account. Additionally, phishing attacks may trick an individual into entering their authentication code.

    A physical security key is the most secure, as it is not at risk of phishing attacks (security keys check the website/application being used in order to ensure they are legitimate. Although the most secure they are also the least convenient, due to requiring the user to have a physical object to sign-in.

    Although an individual might think more authentication options will make them more secure, it is actually the opposite. An individual is better off with only one or two authentication options as more options may put user at higher risk.
